## Executive Summary Template

## 📝 Context

Use this when you need to turn technical progress into an executive-ready update. The goal is to make the outcome, decision, and business impact obvious in under two minutes.

Executives do not need the full implementation narrative. They need to know what changed, why it matters, what risk remains, and what decision or next step is required.

## 📋 Executive Summary Structure

**1. Outcome**
- What was accomplished?
- Which business objective did it advance?
- What changed from the previous status?

**2. Evidence**
- What proof do we have?
- Which success criteria, workflow, metric, or stakeholder validation confirms it?
- What customer quote, sign-off, test result, or operational signal supports it?

**3. Business Impact**
- Did this unblock revenue, reduce risk, accelerate timeline, or improve confidence?
- What cost, delay, operational burden, or adoption risk was reduced?
- What decision is now easier for the customer to make?

**4. Remaining Risk**
- What is still open?
- Who owns it?
- When will it be resolved?

**5. Ask / Next Step**
- What decision, approval, resource, or meeting is needed?
- What happens next if the ask is approved?

## 🎯 Copy-Paste Template

**Executive Summary:** [One sentence describing the business outcome]

**Progress Since Last Update:**
- [Outcome achieved]
- [Evidence collected]
- [Stakeholder/customer validation]

**Business Impact:**
- [Revenue, adoption, risk, timeline, or operational impact]
- [Why this matters to the customer or deal]

**Remaining Risk / Open Items:**
- [Risk or dependency] — Owner: [Name] — ETA: [Date]
- [Risk or dependency] — Owner: [Name] — ETA: [Date]

**Recommended Next Step:** [Decision, approval, follow-up, or handoff]

## ✅ Customer-Facing Examples

### Technical Blocker Resolved

**Executive Summary:** The integration blocker has been resolved, allowing the POC to continue against the original decision timeline.

**Progress Since Last Update:**
- Resolved the authentication mismatch between [Customer System] and [Product].
- Validated end-to-end login with three representative user roles.
- Confirmed with [Customer Technical Owner] that the workflow now meets the access requirement.

**Business Impact:**
- Keeps the evaluation on track for the [Decision Date] review.
- Reduces the risk of delayed rollout caused by identity integration uncertainty.
- Gives the security and platform teams confidence that the solution can fit the existing operating model.

**Remaining Risk / Open Items:**
- Document production identity configuration — Owner: [Name] — ETA: [Date]

**Recommended Next Step:** Proceed with the remaining workflow validation and schedule the POC recap with the executive sponsor.

### POC Success Criteria Achieved

**Executive Summary:** The POC has met the agreed success criteria, giving the team the evidence needed to move from technical validation to purchase decision.

**Progress Since Last Update:**
- Validated [X] of [Y] agreed success criteria.
- Demonstrated [Primary Use Case] using customer-provided data.
- Captured screenshots, test results, and stakeholder feedback for the final recap.

**Business Impact:**
- Confirms the solution can support the target business workflow.
- Reduces evaluation uncertainty for technical, security, and business stakeholders.
- Creates a clear basis for the next commercial and implementation discussion.

**Remaining Risk / Open Items:**
- Confirm preferred rollout phase — Owner: [Name] — ETA: [Date]

**Recommended Next Step:** Hold the value recap, confirm success criteria sign-off, and align on procurement and implementation milestones.

### Implementation Risk Reduced

**Executive Summary:** The highest-risk implementation dependency has been validated, reducing uncertainty around deployment readiness.

**Progress Since Last Update:**
- Completed connectivity validation from [Environment] to [Required Service].
- Confirmed firewall and routing requirements with the customer infrastructure team.
- Documented the deployment assumptions that must carry into production.

**Business Impact:**
- Lowers the chance of late-stage implementation delays.
- Gives the customer a more reliable rollout plan and resource estimate.
- Helps leadership make a decision with fewer unknowns around operational readiness.

**Remaining Risk / Open Items:**
- Production change window approval — Owner: [Name] — ETA: [Date]

**Recommended Next Step:** Move to implementation planning and include the validated network requirements in the project plan.

### Time-to-Value Accelerated

**Executive Summary:** The team shortened the path from kickoff to first validated outcome by reusing the standard deployment checklist and pre-validating access.

**Progress Since Last Update:**
- Completed environment readiness before the kickoff.
- Deployed the core workflow in [X] days instead of the planned [Y] days.
- Validated the first business use case ahead of schedule.

**Business Impact:**
- Pulls forward the customer’s first measurable value milestone.
- Creates more time in the POC window for stakeholder validation and expansion use cases.
- Improves confidence that implementation can meet the customer’s target go-live date.

**Remaining Risk / Open Items:**
- Confirm whether to use saved time for additional validation or earlier final recap — Owner: [Name] — ETA: [Date]

**Recommended Next Step:** Reconfirm priorities with the customer sponsor and use the accelerated timeline to strengthen the final value case.

## ⚠️ Gotchas

- Leading with activities instead of outcomes: “configured SSO” is weaker than “removed the security blocker to adoption.”
- Overloading executives with logs, commands, or implementation details.
- Claiming business impact without evidence.
- Hiding remaining risk. Executives trust clear ownership more than vague optimism.
- Forgetting the ask. Every executive summary should make the next decision easier.
