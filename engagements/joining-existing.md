## ğŸ¯ Getting Up to Speed

**Understand the Current State**

- What stage is the engagement? (Discovery, POC, implementation, post-sale?)
- What's been completed so far?
- What's currently in progress?
- What are the open issues or blockers?
- Who are the key stakeholders? (Customer and internal)

**Review Existing Work**

- Read all status updates and trip reports
- Review technical documentation and architecture notes
- Check for any handoff documents
- Review customer communication history
- Understand what's been promised or committed

**Clarify Your Role**

- What are you responsible for? (Specific tasks, customer contacts, technical areas)
- Who else is involved and what do they own?
- What's the communication model? (Daily standups, weekly syncs, async updates?)
- Who makes decisions? (You, lead SE, team consensus?)

## ğŸ¯ Integration Steps

**Week 1: Observe and Learn**

1. **Attend existing meetings** - Don't skip customer calls or internal syncs
   - Listen more than you speak initially
   - Understand the dynamic and communication style
   - Note who the key decision makers are

2. **Review technical work** - Understand what's been deployed or configured
   - Review architecture and configuration
   - Understand the environment setup
   - Know what's working and what's not

3. **Build relationships** - Introduce yourself appropriately
   - Customer: "I'm joining to help with [X], looking forward to working together"
   - Internal: Sync with lead SE and understand how they work

4. **Document gaps** - Note what you don't understand yet
   - Ask questions in internal channels (not customer-facing)
   - Fill in your knowledge gaps
   - Don't pretend to know things you don't

**Week 2+: Start Contributing**

- Take ownership of specific tasks or areas
- Communicate proactively about your work
- Don't duplicate what others are doing
- Escalate blockers or questions appropriately

- Assuming you understand everything - ask questions early
